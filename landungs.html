Landungsbrücken

The Landungsbrücken are located in the Hamburg district of St. Pauli between Niederhafen and St. Pauli Fischmarkt on the River Elbe and are named after the district. - The street Bei den St. Pauli-Landungsbrücken runs along the shore side of the Landungsbrücken. At the western end of the multi-storey, landside building complex is the northern entrance to the Old Elbe Tunnel in a separate domed building. The eastern end of the building complex is formed by the tide gauge tower. Halfway up the tower on the Elbe side, a water level indicator is embedded in the top of the wall to provide information on the current tide level. In its second function, the water level tower is also a clock and bell tower. The clock face is visible from afar. The bell rings on the hour and half hour to the rhythm of the sea chimes. Opposite the eastern end of the jetties, almost in line with them, is the overseas bridge on a separate pontoon facility, which was formerly the landing stage for passenger ships travelling to and from ports abroad. The cargo motor ship Cap San Diego is also permanently moored here as a museum ship. Hamburg Landungsbrücken station consists of an underground station, an S-Bahn station and bus stops, making it a transport hub in Hamburg. The Landungsbrücken are also a tourist attraction, as they offer an interesting view of the Norderelbe, the shipyards opposite and the harbour basin.
